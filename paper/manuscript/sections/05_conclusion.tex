\section{Conclusion}

\sys frames retrieval infrastructure for agentic AI as an auditable deployment decision rather than a leaderboard. Its decision cards report only conformance-gated configurations and keep quality, post-insert retrievability, \maxrowp latency, context cost, budget stability, objective sensitivity, and paired margins visible. The results show why this matters: objective choice, tail-latency tie-breaks, paired audits, and budget effects all change how a retrieval-memory deployment should be read.
